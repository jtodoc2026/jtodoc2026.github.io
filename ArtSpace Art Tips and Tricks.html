Art Tips and Tricks

Tip#1: Explore Different Mediums

In the art field, there are a variety of art mediums you can use to create a piece. From charcoal and graphite pencils to watercolor, acrylic, and oil paints (and definitely many more). During these modern times, there is also an addition of digital art to the different art mediums. Among these varieties of art mediums, it is very important to know which art medium you work the best with. I, in particular, use watercolor and gouache combined because I feel the most comfortable with it. However, I didn’t immediately know that these mediums were “the one”. I went through using crayons, oil pastels, colored pencils and markers, oil paint, poster color, and digital art, though all of them did not sit that well with me. This is why try to experiment and explore. Find the medium you're good at, improve your strengths, then once you've already mastered that medium, try experimenting with more mediums or challenge yourself by using the mediums you have difficulty handling. You never know what great things could happen if you don't try.

Tip#2: Don't Be Afraid to Make Mistakes

It’s alright to have artworks that don’t work out. It's okay if some of your artworks are not up to your expectations. It's okay to throw away pieces that you aren't satisfied with. Art is also a form of experimentation, so just be patient and things will work out soon ^^

Tip#3: Create Your Own Art Style

If you're trying to find your art style, you can try gathering and combining the specific features that you about your favorite artist's art style then you can add your own preferences and style to make it your own.

Tip#4: Learn From Other Artists

If you don't know where or how to start, try looking at the artworks of other artists and allow yourself to be inspired. There are tens and thousands of techniques, tips, and tricks that you can learn from them.

Tip#5: Practice, Practice, and Practice

Simply knowing the concepts behind great art isn't enough to create great art. Practice or applying these concepts is essential because there are some things and techniques that can only be realized or achieved through practice. Making art journals is one way to do it. You can either draw your surroundings, a simple doodle, or have daily/weekly art challenges to enhance your creativity and skill.

Tip#6: Don't Procrastinate

It's not only in academics where procrastinating is discouraged, it's also in art. Try starting a piece as soon as you have an idea. Leaving your ideas on your waiting list will eventually cause you to be less motivated in doing it.

Tip#7: Use References

Some may say that drawings with references are unoriginal or considered cheating. However, references actually play a big part in our development as artists. By simply observing and drawing the objects surrounding uou we get to improve our fundamentals in art such as form, shape, shadow, proportions, perspective, and more! We also get to see the world in a different and more detailed way.

Tip#8: Use Good Materials to Make Good Art

Practicing and developing art skills is important, but to take artwork to the next level, make sure to use good quality materials and the right mediums on the right surfaces. I ensure you that it will make a huge difference in the outcome of your artwork.

Tip#9: Do Not Compare Yourself To Others

It's fine to look at the work of other artists for inspiration, but try not to let it demotivate you by comparing your own work to theirs. Every person has a unique, individual style. Everyone's pace of artistic development also differs from one person to another. 

Tip#10: Have Fun!

Lastly, a friendly reminder to have fun doing what you love! Don't forget to trust and enjoy the process while you're at it!
